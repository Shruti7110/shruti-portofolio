01 / About

Engineer at the
intersection of Hardware & AI

I'm an Electronics Engineer who loves building systems at the boundary of hardware and intelligence.

I started at Baxter International, working on medical device infusion systems — redesigning PCBAs, validating component alternatives, and debugging real-time firmware using UART diagnostics and logic analyzers. I built Hardware-in-Loop (HIL) setups to automate sensor signal simulation for regression testing, and developed a real-time sensor dashboard for field diagnostics. That role gave me a solid foundation in electronics, embedded validation, and structured engineering processes. But I wanted to go deeper into firmware — not just validate it, but write it from scratch.

So I stepped away and committed to upskilling. I dove into bare-metal embedded development: writing AVR firmware without libraries, implementing custom bootloaders, and deploying TinyML models on resource-constrained microcontrollers. I trained gesture recognition models with Edge Impulse and deployed them on ATmega328P hardware, learning edge AI at the silicon level.

I also expanded into Generative AI and agentic systems — building AI-powered automation tools using MCP, LLMs, and Python. I've delivered multiple freelance projects applying AI to real engineering workflows.
